Unlocking the Secrets of a Longer Life

What Is Life Expectancy?

Life expectancy is the average number of years a person or population is expected to live and is a crucial metric for assessing overall health and well-being. It's influenced by factors like healthcare access, socioeconomic status, genetics, lifestyle choices, and public health measures. Improvements in these areas have contributed to increased life expectancies in many regions, highlighting the significance of addressing these determinants for healthier and longer lives.

World Life Expectancy (2000-2015)

Life expectancy varies significantly among countries, with the highest recorded at around 85 years and the lowest at approximately 50 years. The map illustrates a gradual increase in life expectancy from 2000 to 2015. Despite this upward trend, Africa has consistently displayed the lowest life expectancy among continents due to a combination of factors such as prevalent infectious diseases, limited healthcare access, and socio-economic hurdles. Within Asia, Japan stands out with the highest life expectancy. However, regions in America and Europe surpass these figures with notably higher life expectancies.

The bubble plot shows a possitive correlation between income composition of resources and life expectancy. When the income composition of resources increases, the life expectancy of the continent also increases. From the size of the bubble plot, we can see that the GDP and life expectancy has a possitive correlation as well. With that, life expectancy will increase when the GDP of the continents increases. In conclusion, we can increase the life exepectancy by increaseing the income composition of resources and GDP by implementing a combination of strategies including economic diversification, industrialization, human capital development, and infrastructure improvement.

Effect On Immunization Coverage Among 1 Year Olds (%)

The parallel coordinate plot illustrates a positive correlation between life expectancy and the percentage of immunization coverage among 1 year olds. This relationship is primarily attributed to the protective benefits of vaccines, which effectively safeguard overall health by preventing a range of life-threatening diseases and severe health complications. Higher immunization rates among children not only curtail the transmission of these diseases within the population but also yield several key factors that underlie the observed increase in life expectancy.
